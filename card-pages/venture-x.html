Calculate Your Annual Fee

The Venture X has a $395 annual fee, but it comes with up to $400 in travel credits that are relatively easy to use, plus TSA PreCheck/Global Entry. Those credits are basically built into the annual fee—you're prepaying for them.

If you can use those travel credits effectively, the card's perks and protections become a great bonus. If not, you're just paying for benefits you won't use. The key is figuring out what these credits are actually worth to you.

Annual Travel Credit
Use up to $300 each account year on bookings made through Capital One Travel (flights, hotels, rental cars). At checkout, the portal shows your remaining credit and applies it to reduce the price you pay—no separate rebate later. You can split it across multiple bookings until you hit $300; unused credit doesn't roll over. If a booking is refunded, the used portion of the credit is typically returned to your balance once the refund posts, and you can apply it again within the same account year. TSA PreCheck / Global Entry Credit
Receive a statement credit up to $125 when you pay the application fee for TSA PreCheck (every 4.5 years) or Global Entry (every 5 years) with this card. The credit typically posts within 1–2 statements after the charge. It covers one application fee per window and can be used for any traveler you pay for (not just the cardholder). Approval isn't guaranteed by Capital One, but the credit applies whether you're approved or denied. (CLEAR Plus isn't included.) Since Global Entry is $125 every 5 years, this credit is worth about $25 per year on average.
