Practical Recommendation

The Sapphire Reserve is a high-fee, high-effort lifestyle card. It works best if your normal routine already hits several of the available credits—dining out at partner spots, live events, delivery/ride share, streaming—with some travel sprinkled in. We would only recommend this card if you really know what you are getting into.

Credits

  • $300 Annual Travel Credit (broad travel; auto-statement credit on qualifying travel each account year)
  • $500 The Edit Hotel Credit ($250 Jan–Jun + $250 Jul–Dec; prepaid, 2-night minimum; bookings don't earn points)
  • $300 Dining Credit at Sapphire Reserve Exclusive Tables ($150 twice a year; book via OpenTable)
  • $300 Live Events Credit for StubHub/viagogo ($150 twice a year)
  • $250 Apple TV+ & Apple Music subscriptions
  • DashPass membership + $300 in monthly promos ($25/mo)
  • Lyft: $10/mo ride credits + 5x on rides
  • Peloton: $10/mo membership credits + 10x on eligible equipment
  • Global Entry/TSA PreCheck/NEXUS fee credit up to $120 every 4 years

Perks

Premium travel benefits and access with the Chase Sapphire Reserve:

  • Lounge access: Priority Pass Select (1,300+ lounges) + Chase Sapphire Lounges, with up to 2 guests; also access to select Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounges/Cafés with an eligible boarding pass
  • Hotel status: IHG One Rewards Platinum Elite through 12/31/2027
  • Points Boost (redemption): on select flights/hotels in Chase Travel, points can be worth up to 2× (watch for the "Points Boost" tag)
  • No foreign transaction fees
  • Reserve Travel Designers trip-planning service (valued up to $300 per trip)

Protections

Insurance and security features included:

  • Primary rental car CDW up to $75,000 (worldwide)
  • Trip cancel/interruption up to $10,000 per traveler / $20,000 per trip
  • Trip delay (6+ hrs/overnight) up to $500 per traveler
  • Baggage delay (6+ hrs) $100/day up to 5 days; lost/damaged luggage up to $3,000 per traveler
  • Emergency evacuation up to $100,000; emergency medical/dental up to $2,500 ($50 deductible)
  • Roadside assistance up to $50 per incident (4×/yr)
  • Travel accident insurance up to $1,000,000
  • Purchase protection (up to $10,000/item, 120 days), return protection (up to $500/item), extended warranty (+1 year on eligible warranties)

Calculate Your Annual Fee

The new Sapphire Reserve can be incredible value—if your normal spending already lines up with its maze of credits (travel, Edit hotels, dining list, events, Apple, DoorDash, Lyft, Peloton… you get it). If you're jumping through hoops or buying stuff you wouldn't otherwise, the "$2,000+ in value" evaporates fast.

Toggle only the credits you'll actually use with organic spend, adjust the dollar amounts to what they're worth to you, and see how much of the annual fee you truly offset. You can learn more about how to value credits here. This is a high-effort, high-fee card—amazing for the right person, unnecessary for many. Let the math, not the marketing, make the decision.

DoorDash Monthly Promos
You get $25 per month split into three separate credits: $5 for one restaurant order + $10 for two non-restaurant orders (groceries/convenience). To use the full $25, you must place three separate orders in the same month: one restaurant order (uses the $5) and two non-restaurant orders (each uses a $10). Credits don't stack on one order and don't roll over. They typically don't cover taxes/fees/tips. Make sure the merchant is tagged correctly in DoorDash (restaurant vs. grocery/convenience), activate DashPass, pay with your Sapphire Reserve, and the credits auto-apply at checkout if eligible. Example: $12 lunch uses the $5; two $20 grocery runs each use a $10. Obviously this is a lot of work to redeem a credit. If you are already a frequent Door Dasher, can be some value, but if you don't already use Door Dash value this at $0.
